## Table Notes

Table 1 reports analytic cohorts with known primary endpoint status: NHANES one-year mortality, MIMIC-IV ICU hospital mortality, and eICU hospital mortality. NHANES CKD is defined from measured eGFR and/or albuminuria, and NHANES hypertension/CVD use survey or examination-derived history variables; MIMIC-IV/eICU CKD, hypertension, and CVD are harmonized from diagnosis/history indicators. ICU laboratory and vital-sign variables are first values within 24 hours after ICU admission.

Table 2 reports source-classifier AUCs. Source classification is contrast-level, not directional; the MIMIC-IV ICU vs eICU contrast applies to both ICU transport directions.

Table 3 reports unweighted logistic transport performance with percentile 95% bootstrap intervals. Endpoint differences are explicit; cross-scenario comparison should focus on failure mode and recalibration behavior rather than endpoint-equivalent absolute accuracy. Row-to-row differences in AUC, ECE, or Brier score should not be interpreted as absolute performance differences under a shared endpoint.

Table 4 reports ECE with empirical 95% intervals across repeated local recalibration samples. Raw transport rows are point estimates before local recalibration.

Table 5 reports unweighted raw transported logistic-regression performance in clinically defined subgroups. ECE is the 10-bin equal-frequency expected calibration error.

Table 6 reports selected-threshold decision-curve net benefit for raw transport, intercept-only 100-event updating, Platt 100-event updating, and the internal target-site HGB benchmark.
